Frequently Asked Questions

Is this coin flip truly random?
Yes. We use JavaScript's cryptographically-seeded Math.random() function which produces statistically fair 50/50 results. Each flip is completely independent of the last.
What are the odds of getting heads 10 times in a row?
The probability is (1/2)^10 = 1 in 1,024. While rare, it's perfectly possible. Each flip is independent — past results never affect future flips.
Can I flip multiple coins at once?
Yes! Enter a number in the "Flip Multiple Coins" section and click the button. You can flip up to 100 coins simultaneously.
What is the history of coin flipping?
Coin flipping dates back to ancient Rome where it was called "navia aut caput" (ship or head). It has been used for centuries to make fair decisions when two parties cannot agree.
Can I use this for sports decisions or games?
Absolutely. This tool is perfect for settling disputes, deciding who goes first in a game, making random choices, or any situation requiring a fair 50/50 decision.
